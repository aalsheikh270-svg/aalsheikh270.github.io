About Me

Soccer Player & Creator

01
My Journey

Where It Started

I started playing soccer in grade 2, and it quickly became one of my favorite activities. There's something special about being on the field: the teamwork, the competition, the feeling when you make a great pass or score a goal.

Over the years, soccer has taught me a lot. Not just about the sport itself, but about working with others, staying committed to something, and always trying to improve. Even when things don't go perfectly, there's always something to learn from each game.

The Connection to Equipment

My time playing soccer also made me curious about the equipment side of sports. I started noticing how different cleats affected ball control, how proper gear could make a difference in performance, and how innovation in sports technology keeps evolving.

02
The Project

Why AeroStride?

For this project, I wanted to combine my interest in soccer with learning about business and web design. Creating AeroStride as a conceptual sports brand let me explore how real sports companies think about their products, marketing, and customer experience.

I researched current trends in athletic footwear, learned about technologies like custom fitting and advanced materials, and thought about what athletes actually need from their gear based on my own experience playing soccer.

This project gave me a chance to think creatively about how sports brands connect with athletes and what makes equipment truly valuable to the people who use it.

03
What I Bring

My Approach

Athlete's Perspective

Playing soccer since grade 2 gives me real insight into what athletes look for in their gear. I understand the importance of comfort, fit, and performance because I've experienced it firsthand on the field.

Love for the Game

My philosophy is simple: try your best and enjoy the game, rather than just focusing on winning. This mindset helps me appreciate the journey and the process, whether in sports or in projects like this one.

Research & Learning

For this project, I dove into learning about sports technology, business strategies, and web design. Understanding how real companies innovate helped me create a more authentic and detailed concept for AeroStride.

Attention to Detail

From the product descriptions to the custom fit technology concept, I tried to make every part of this website feel realistic and well thought out. Small details matter, both in sports and in design.

Creative Thinking

This project challenged me to think like an entrepreneur and designer: imagining what a sports brand could be, how it would communicate with customers, and what would make it stand out in a competitive market.

My Philosophy

"Try your best and enjoy the game: that's what matters most."

This is the mindset I learned from playing soccer, and it's how I approached this project. It's not just about the final result, but about putting in genuine effort, learning along the way, and enjoying the creative process.

AeroStride represents my passion for soccer, my curiosity about business and innovation, and my commitment to creating something I can be proud of for this school project.
